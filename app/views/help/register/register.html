How can I register a record in the ABS-CH?

In order to register information, users must have a accounts.cbd.int to choose the category of information they wish to submit from the [list of pre-defined information types] or common formats. The categories of information that a registered user is able to submit depends on their designated role in the ABS Clearing-House. National records can only be registered by national authorized users (NAUs) or publishing authority (PA) duly designated by a country. However, any registered user will be able to register reference records in the Virtual Library. Once you are signed in the ABS-CH you will find on the left panel the list of records that can be registered. By selecting one of the categories from the list you will find information on this type of record including the status of your work on similar records within the same category. You can click in the button “new” to add a new record. Your dashboard will show the status of the records. A CBD account can be setup through accounts.cbd.int and gives you access to manage information registered under the different CBD Clearing-Houses including the BCH, ABS-CH and CHM.

What is the maximum size for the attachments? Do accounts have a storage capacity limit?

The maximum size for attachments is 10 Megabytes. There is no limit for how many records a user may include in the ABS-CH.

How can I save information without publishing it?

Users are able to save information without publishing it when saving draft records. Draft records may be saved in any category for editing at a later moment by clicking the button “save as a draft” in the bar located over the form.

What kind of information can be registered in the Virtual Library?

The Virtual Library can host a wide variety of resources relevant to access and benefit-sharing and implementation of the Nagoya Protocol, including books, documents, capacity-building materials, meeting or conference proceedings, videos, news services, websites and databases, and others.
The Virtual Library is also hosting information on model contractual clauses, codes of conduct, guidelines and best practices and/or standards. Indigenous and local communities can also share through this platform relevant ABS information, including community protocols.
When registering a record, users are encouraged to select the relevant thematic areas for the resource being included. The thematic areas selected will determine how the record will be displayed when conducting searches in the ABS-CH.

How do I edit records? Can I duplicate records?

To edit or duplicate a record you need to:

  • Go to the page “register information”
  • Select the category of record you may wish to edit or duplicate.
  • In your dashboard you will find a list of all records drafted or published within this category. Next to each record you will find three buttons that will allow you to duplicate, edit or delete the record.

For how long can I keep a record as a draft?

There is no limitation of time for having a record in draft form.
