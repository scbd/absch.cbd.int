Step-By-Step Guides

The Secretariat has prepared Step-By-Step Guides to assist you with creating and managing your CBD account, user management in the ABS Clearing-House, publishing national contacts, national records and reference records. The Step-By-Step Guides on national and reference records will provide you a detailed overview of the common formats, mandatory information, and guidance on filling the forms.


Creating and Managing your CBD Account While the information published in the ABS Clearing-House can be seen by anyone, in order to publish records it is necessary to create a CBD account. This guide will provide detailed assistance in creating and managing a CBD account.


User Management in the ABS Clearing-House If you are the national focal point (NFP) on access and benefit-sharing for your country, you can designate the PA and one or more NAUs through the ABS Clearing-House. Also, if you are the PA, you can designate the NAUs through the ABS Clearing-House. This guide will offer assistance in granting or revoking these roles online through the ABS Clearing-House.


National records


Publishing Competent National Authorities (CNA) CNAs are entities designated to, in accordance with applicable national legislative, administrative or policy measures, be responsible for granting access or, as applicable, issuing written evidence that access requirements have been met and be responsible for advising on applicable procedures and requirements for obtaining prior informed consent and entering into mutually agreed terms. The Step-By-Step Guide will assist you with publishing information on your CNAs.


Updating legislative, administrative and policy measures on access and benefit sharing (MSR) The common format for legislative, administrative or policy measures (ABS measures) was revised in 2016 to include: a modified list of types of measures, reference to model contractual clauses, and a modified list of ABS elements. As a result, previously published records of ABS measures should be updated according to the revised common format. This Step-By-Step Guide will provide assistance in updating previously published ABS measures.


Reference records


Publishing Capacity-Building Resources Capacity-building resources are made available through the ABSCH’s virtual library. Records in the virtual library are not limited to capacity-building resources. They include a variety of information related to ABS, such as: general literature and other resources meant for awareness-raising, training, as well as capacity-building. Although any user with a CBD account can submit reference records, including capacity-building resources, the SCBD is responsible for validating and publishing them in the ABSCH.


Publishing Model Contractual Clauses, Codes of Conduct, Guidelines, Best practices and Standards Articles 19 and 20 of the Nagoya Protocol require each Party to encourage, as appropriate, the development, update and use of sectoral and cross-sectoral model contractual clauses for mutually agreed terms, as well as voluntary codes of conduct, guidelines and best practices and/or standards in relation to access and benefit sharing. With this Step-By-Step Guide, you will receive assistance in submitting information through the ABS Clearing-House.


Publishing Capacity-Building Initiatives Information on Capacity-building Initiatives at national, regional and international levels, are shared to promote synergy and coordination on capacity-building and development for access and benefit-sharing. This Step-By-Step Guide provides assistance in submitting information on CBIs through the ABS Clearing-House.
